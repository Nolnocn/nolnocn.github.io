About

I spent a hefty portion of my childhood with a controller in my hand. Admittedly, I was never particularly good at games, but that didn't stop me or diminish my enjoyment. I still remember my friend's older sister had to show me how to save my progress in Pok√©mon Red on the school bus, so I could even venture beyond Pallet Town. It was the challenges and figuring things out as I went along (even if I needed some help every now and then) that I loved most about games.

Funnily enough, it wasn't actually playing games that drove me into game development... it was a high school math course. As much as I loved using geometry and trigonometry to prove silly mathematical identities for tests, the realization that I could apply these same principles to move a character or fire a missile in some direction opened up a whole new world of possibilities. Just like that, the challenge wasn't figuring out how to save my own progress anymore, it was about figuring out how I could save someone else's.

For me, game development isn't some lofty dream of taking wild ideas and making them reality. It's an opportunity to tackle unique challenges with nothing but my wits, an evergrowing bag of coding tricks, and a few Google searches. Every system, feature, and bug I create is a learning experience, a chance to grow and prepare for the next challenge. Plus, I get to work on games, which is pretty awesome.
